How to Play

🃏

The Goal

Beat the dealer by getting a hand value as close to 21 as possible without going over (busting).

🔢

Card Values

Number cards = face value. Face cards (J, Q, K) = 10. Ace = 1 or 11 — whichever is better for your hand.

🎯

Your Options

  • Hit – draw another card
  • Stand – keep your current hand
  • Double Down – double your bet, receive exactly one more card
🏦

Dealer Rules

The dealer must hit on 16 or less and stand on 17 or more. One dealer card starts face-down.

💰

Payouts

  • Win → 1:1
  • Push (tie) → Bet back
  • Blackjack → 3:2
♠

Blackjack!

An Ace + a 10-value card on the first two cards = Blackjack. Pays 3:2 unless the dealer also has Blackjack (push).

Pro Tips

01

Never take Insurance

Insurance is a side bet that pays 2:1 if the dealer has blackjack. The long-run expected value is negative — always decline it.

02

Always Double on 11

With a total of 11, doubling against any dealer upcard except an Ace gives you the highest mathematical edge.

03

Stand on soft 18 vs. 2–6

A,7 (soft 18) is a strong hand against dealer upcards 2 through 6. Double if you can; otherwise stand.

04

Manage Your Bankroll

Bet 1–5% of your bankroll per hand. This keeps you in the game through variance swings and lets strategy work over time.

05

Ignore "Hot Streaks"

Each hand is independent. Don't increase bets after wins or decrease after losses based on gut feel — stick to the chart.

06

Hit 16 vs. Dealer 7+

A hard 16 against dealer 7 through Ace is one of the worst hands in blackjack. Hit — standing is statistically worse.
